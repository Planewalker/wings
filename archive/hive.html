“If there’s a blame, find a tiefling, and if the tiefling didn’t do it, he was probably pressed for time.”
-Common Sigilian proverb

It's not easy being a spiv in the nexus of the planes, but I don't complain. ’Course, the word ‘spiv’ is often associated with ‘thief’, and Sigil has more words for thief than anything else: cross-trader, cony-catcher, jink-peeler, cutpurse, knight of the post, peel master. But if you're called a spiv it just means you get by as you need. It shouldn't make a difference in the end. It's more dignifying to call yourself a spiv than a thief, or a whore (a ‘honey-peeler’ if you're both). You bob the gullies when you find ’em, dead-book ’em if you have to, turn stag on your buddies to save yourself, and put out when you have to. There’s more than enough clueless sods in Sigil, and they’ve got more jink than they need. They come and go every day from all over the multiverse, so who knows the difference? They might get penned in the dead-book anyway.

They worry more about pick-pocketing than thuggery or scrubbing in the Clerk’s Ward. Women have it easier there ‘cause most men don’t think they can get bobbed by a lady, and most ladies don’t expect a sister to peel ’em. It's easy to peel a mark if you’re some high-up doxy who can charm gullies with more jink than those bubbers in the Hive, but harder if you're Hive trash yourself. Harder still if you're a tiefling. Not to say that doesn't also have it's pluses. Some gullies that see me are more fascinated than afraid, or simply barmy, so if they've got much jink I play into that. Mayhap they want to know what it's like to bed a woman who has a tail, or they're curious about what type of devil's blood runs through my veins. Or I just beat it out of them instead. Sometimes I'm not very subtle.

The other girls hate me, though, ’cause I'm more straightforward while they're teasers. I kick the shit out of them too. They hate me even more if they're plane-touched like me, ’cause another tiefling makes the competition difficult. Well, usually, anyway. Nherid hated me for a while. ’Course, when she realized now easily I could pen her in the dead-book her she backed off. Then she found out I was a member of her faction, the Xaositects, so she started helping me to learn a thing or two about the cross-trade. And though she's addle-coved and inexperienced, she follows me around and does everything I do. We learned that two tieflings can sometimes pull a better bob than one, so now we share our jink. And men.

Some men are good thieves, too, like Ruzzier. He's smart and calm and stays out of my way, and he's good like that. He was Nherid's lover first, but now we both have him. It works out well for all of us. Nherid gets to be a leech as that's her lot in life, Ruzzier gets to fuck two tieflings instead of one (I’m not into the buggery as much as he is, though), and both of them know I'm in charge. It sure beats a ménage a trois with two men.

And Ruzzier’s well-lanned. He’s got a friend named Geordie who’s better dressed than the rest of us. He can go into the Clerk’s Ward and pretend to be some bureaucrat. We don’t go in there too often, ’cause it’s well guarded, but Geordie’s twigged to the idea of scanning the blocks, finding a rich case, and giving us the directions. Then he sends the real scrubbers in-that’d be me, Valese, and Nherid. We’re tiefling sisters-in- work. And of course Ruzzier’s our sharpshooter. We’ll relieve these sods of their jink. No one expects Hivers to knock you off in the Clerk’s Ward, and no one wants to follow them back to where they came from.

Tonight we stand in front of a tottering sooty wall. It’s called Founder’s Fence. They built it to try and keep the Clerk’s Ward safe from the corrupting influences of the Hive. It’s crumbled in a few places, and Geordie showed us a breach in the wall we can all squeeze through. There are no Hardheads in sight, so we’ll amble through and into the ward. I’m the fastest, so I’m first to bolt through the open streets. The clean streets are wide, their granite cobbles painted bright blue and green, and there’s no traffic. The nearest spikeward street’s even lit by a tall lamppost. I kinda want to smash it too. I don’t need lights to see in the dark. Neither does Nherid. Ruzzier doesn’t see in infra the way we do, so that’d be a mistake. ’Course, I can see lights from all over Sigil in the sky too, ‘cause Sigil’s a big sodding ring.

With no Harmonium officers in sight, I point the way and we sprint quickly and quietly as we can, to the house Geordie found for us. “Geordie said the couple that owns this place’d be gone.” Ruzzier hisses.

“Nothing,” he says.

“Well,” I shrug, “I guess this is for not having much jink.”

Frothy blood’s gushing out of his mouth though his face is otherwise white as an eggshell, but I don’t give him time to die. Ruzzier comes along and helps me bring him in, though when he looks back he signals to me that he sees some folks coming. Nherid and Ruzzier are already on their way, but I kick the graybeard in the ribs before I run out so I can hear his pathetic mewling again. They’re already ahead of me, but I still catch up to them. We run down the open street, on our way back to Founder’s Fence. I look back, and I can see the hardheads approaching under the shadows, but I don’t think they see us. If we can make the corner before--

They won’t chase us in here unless they’re really barmy. ’Course, they don’t care what Hivers do to each other; they only see to it that we don’t bob the high-ups in the Clerk’s Ward.

The empty, three- walled buildings around us are half filled with rubble and refuse, home only to scavengers and dwindling families. The skies above are covered in a greasy smog, the alleys are clogged with brackish pools of garbage. The streets are narrow rows of ramshackle slant-roofed tenements and belching smokestacks. “Do you have to go?”

Jesper nods grimly.

“It's not my decision,” he says.

“What’s your world like, you never told me much about it?”

“Well...there’s not much to say, really,” Jesper says uneasily, “It’s not a bad place, the scholars of my city have an interest in the planes and planar magic, but I’ve been... sheltered and isolated most of my life. For a moment, it almost looks like Jesper’s about to cry himself.

“Of course.”

I put my arm around Jesper and rest my head against his shoulder. I fall asleep and dream of hummingbirds.

I wake up a few times early in the morning, and I’m dimly aware Jesper is up gathering things, but I’m so tired I fall asleep again. When I get up he’s gone, but the candle and lamp are still there, and on the desk is a note, a handful of gold coins, and a thick string.

Valese-

You were sleeping so peacefully it would have been a crime to wake you. I’ve left some money (or is it jink?) behind for you, and I’m on my way back to the Prime. I wish you well with everything. Please don’t think you are unworthy of love, or devoid of responsibility. I think there’s more good in you than you realize. I enjoyed my stay in Sigil, and should I ever return I look forward to meeting you again.

I crumple the letter. So easy for him to say that when he can return to his world at any time and I’m stuck here in the Hive. What kind of daft cunt does he think I am? I don’t even know where his portal is, or the key. It angers me more every second. But... I’d like to think I could change... someday... if things were different, mayhap. I’ll record my memory of Jesper at the Civic Festhall. It’ll still be there whenever I need to return to it. Then I’ll find Nherid in the Lower Ward and we’ll bob some clueless sods. It should be fun.

I pocket the jink, use the string to re-lace my bodice, don my belt, sheathe my chivs, and head out.
